How are Ecosystems Affected?

OVERFISHING PUTS SPECIES AT RISK

One third of all shark and ray species are endangered from overfishing.

Overfishing creates competition between humans and sharks, lowering their chance of survival.

In addition, dragnets can catch and tangle sharks, leading to dismemberment or death.

OVERFISHING DISRUPTS ECOSYSTEMS

Dragnet fishing is the practice of catching fish with huge nets that drag along the sea floor.

This practice is very harmful, as the net can destroy ecosystems as it sweeps along.

Fish traps set underwater can damage sensitive enviornments such as coral reefs, which take a long time to recover.

OVERFISHING HURTS REPRODUCTION

Many fish gather together in large populations to mate at predictable times of the year.

Harvesting from these schools greatly hurts the fish population, as it impedes the fish population's ability to rebound.

In addition, nearshore habitats are sometimes vital place for young fish to grow up. Fishing from these environments also removes young fish before they have a chance to grow up and replenish the population.
